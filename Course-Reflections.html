Reflection on Iteration, Recursion, and Backtracking

Iteration, recursion, and backtracking are fundamental concepts in algorithm design. These concepts help solve complex problems in a step-by-step manner. Reflection on their usage in problem-solving has enhanced my understanding of their practical applications.

Reflection on Space and Time Efficiency

Understanding space and time efficiency has been crucial in optimizing algorithms. The concept of time and space complexity and their impact on real-world problems is invaluable.

Reflection on Design Principles

Learning design principles such as pruning, bit manipulation, and edge relaxation has allowed me to make smarter decisions while tackling algorithmic problems. These principles guide the efficient implementation of algorithms.

Reflection on Tree and Graph Data Structures

Tree and graph data structures are essential for solving hierarchical and network-based problems. Understanding their traversal methods and applications in real-world scenarios has been an insightful experience.
